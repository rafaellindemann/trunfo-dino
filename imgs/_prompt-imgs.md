``` 
[DINO_NAME] = 
[DINO_HEIGHT] = 
---
Create a scientific paleoart illustration of a [DINO_NAME].
REAL SCALE:
The dinosaur has an approximate real-world height of [DINO_HEIGHT] meters.
Include a human silhouette with a fixed height of 1.70 meters.
CRITICAL SCALE RULE:
The size ratio must be physically accurate.
Calculate the proportion: [DINO_HEIGHT] ÷ 1.70 and represent it visually.
Do not estimate, stylize, or adjust for composition.
EXAMPLES:
- 2.0m → slightly taller than human
- 4.0m → about 2.3× human height
- very large → human appears very small
COMPOSITION:
- Strict side profile, facing right
- Dinosaur centered horizontally
- Full body visible whenever possible
- Camera at mid-body height
- Orthographic-like view (no perspective distortion)
HUMAN REFERENCE:
- Position: lower right area of the image
- Standing upright on the same ground plane
- Minimal silhouette, solid dark color
- Consistent size and style across all images
IMPORTANT COMPOSITION RULE:
The dinosaur’s real size ALWAYS takes priority over framing.
Do NOT scale or resize the dinosaur to fit the canvas.
If necessary:
- The dinosaur may extend beyond the frame
- The human may appear very small
- The human may be partially cropped
STYLE:
Classic natural history book illustration (DK / museum guide style), consistent across all images.
BACKGROUND:
Light beige/off-white paper tone (#f2efe6 style), with very subtle blurred prehistoric vegetation silhouettes.
GROUND:
Flat ground line, soft shadow under both dinosaur and human, consistent across all images.
LIGHTING:
Soft, even, neutral studio lighting, no dramatic shadows.
TEXTURE:
Clean, slightly simplified, avoid excessive micro-detail.
COLOR:
Natural, desaturated, realistic tones, avoid vivid or saturated colors.
CONSISTENCY:
All images must look like they belong to the same illustrated book.
OUTPUT:
No text, no labels, no watermark.
Resolution around 768x768.
```
